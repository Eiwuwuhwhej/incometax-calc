Know About Inputs

Back to Calculator

This page explains every input field with plain-language examples so freelancers and non-lawyers can use the calculator without confusion.

  • Total Freelance Gross Receipts (₹) All professional fees and invoices billed in the year. Example: if you billed ₹24,00,000, enter 2400000.
  • Amount Received in Cash (₹) Cash payments received from clients. This helps determine Section 44ADA limits. Example: if ₹50,000 was cash, enter 50000.
  • Export Income (₹) Revenue from clients outside India. Example: if ₹6,00,000 came from overseas clients, enter 600000.
  • Actual Business Expenses (₹) Costs such as software, internet, rent, subscriptions. Example: if total expenses are ₹3,00,000, enter 300000.
  • Interest Income (₹) Interest earned on savings or deposits. Example: ₹20,000 earned from a bank FD.
  • Rental Income (₹) Rent you received during the year. Example: ₹1,20,000 from a rented apartment.
  • 80C Deductions (₹) Investments in PPF, ELSS, LIC, etc. Capped at ₹1,50,000. Example: if PPF + ELSS = ₹1,20,000, enter 120000.
  • 80CCD(1B) NPS (₹) Additional NPS deduction beyond 80C. Capped at ₹50,000. Example: if NPS contribution is ₹30,000, enter 30000.
  • 80D Health Insurance (₹) Health insurance premium paid for self/family/senior citizen parents. Capped at ₹50,000. Example: ₹22,000 premium.
  • Section 24b Home Loan Interest (₹) Interest paid on self-occupied home loans. Capped at ₹2,00,000. Example: ₹1,80,000 interest paid.
  • TDS Already Deducted (₹) Tax already withheld by clients on your invoices. Example: if clients have deducted ₹24,000, enter 24000.

How to Submit Your Tax Return

  1. Visit the Income Tax e-filing portal at incometax.gov.in.
  2. Register or login with your PAN and password.
  3. Select the recommended ITR form shown by the calculator.
  4. Fill in income details, deductions, TDS, and bank information.
  5. Verify the return using Aadhaar OTP, EVC, or by sending an ITR-V to CPC Bengaluru.
  6. Pay any balance tax due before filing, and keep the challan number safe.

Documents to Keep Ready

  • Invoices & Receipts All freelancer invoices, expense bills, software subscriptions, coworking rent receipts, and internet bills.
  • Bank Statements Records of income, business spending, interest receipts, and rent receipts.
  • Form 26AS / TDS Certificates Proof of TDS deducted by clients and any other tax credits.
  • Investment Proofs PPF, ELSS, LIC, NPS, and health insurance premium receipts.
  • Home Loan Statement Interest certificate for Section 24b if claiming home loan interest deduction.
  • Export & GST Records Export invoices and GST registration details if applicable.
